  • Where: Bogota, Cali, Manizales
    When: July 9-14, 2012

    Attending Filmmakers: Andrew Okpeah MacLean (writer/director ON THE ICE) & Sofia Santana (Co-Producer BUCK)

    FILM FORWARD will be in Colombia for the first time collaborating with the U.S. Embassy, Colombia, Centro Cultural Colombo Americano, and Proimagenes. Colombia has a long history of film and is experiencing a revived interest in the use of film as a medium to encourage community action and empowerment. The FILM FORWARD Colombia program is particularly relevant as a vehicle to create discussions around powerful films that address important social issues. FILM FORWARD will be traveling to Bogota, Cali, and Manizales with filmmakers Andrew Okpeaha MacLean (On the Ice) and Sofia Santana (Buck). Collectively, their films address the universal theme of empowerment – empowerment to make a choice or to take responsibility for the direction of one’s life. The films illustrate the necessity of compassion and friendship in overcoming the adversity and obstacles that shape one’s life. FILM FORWARD will be reaching students, youth from underprivileged communities, indigenous filmmakers, documentarians, coffee collectors, community leaders, the local film industry, and the general public through screenings, Q&As, workshops, and filmmaker round-tables.

    US Embassy, Bogota, Colombia

    The Embassy in Bogotá is the official mission of the U.S. government in Colombia.  Its officers deal with a variety of topics which unite the two countries, among them political, economic, commercial, military and cultural themes.

    Proimagenes

    The Film Promotion Fund "Proimágenes COLOMBIA" is a non-profit, part of the system of science and technology activities and private entities, and included public and private entities under, rule of law 397 1997, General Law of Culture. COLOMBIA Proimágenes seeks to consolidate and solidify the Colombian film industry, becoming a prime setting for the conclusion of public policies and sectoral, and to articulate rules that materialize and boost the country's film industry. COLOMBIA Proimágenes also administers the Film Development Fund. 

    From a wide range of films, Proimágenes COLOMBIA has become the mediator par excellence of Colombian films in international and domestic markets.  It also promotes the exchange of technology and human resources, research, training and coordinating meetings, exhibitions and festivals related to cine.Desde 2006.  authorized by its board of directors, develops all efforts to operate in Colombia a Film Commission (Film Commission) to serve as a means of brokering and facilitating contact all film industries, tourism and other necessary with all those who are interested in co-production with the country or shoot at.

    Mission: Promoting and strengthening the Colombian film industry as a cultural and education through the creation of mechanisms and support spaces in line with industry needs, with the participation and coordination of public and private entities. 

    Vision: In 2016 Proimagenes will be an internationally prominent institution for knowledge management and promotion of Colombian cinema meeting the highest standards of quality. 

    Colombian/American Bi-national Centers

    The bi-national centers (BNCs) in Colombia are autonomous, non-profit cultural and educational institutions created during the Second World War to contribute to good relations between Colombia and the United States. Several BNCs were founded in 1942. Each month these Centers collectively host 32,500 students of English. In addition, they maintain a slate of cultural activities, a library and US educational advising programs. The BNCs liaison with the Embassy, under the auspices of the Public Affairs Section, in a wide array of mutually beneficial projects, but they do not depend economically on the U.S. government.

    Currently, there are BNCs of different sizes in Armenia, Barranquilla, Bogota, Bucaramanga, Cali, Cartagena, Manizales, Medellin, and Pereira. Some have satellite branches in their cities. Each BNC is managed by a board of directors, generally comprised of Colombian and U.S. nationals resident in Colombia and some have U.S. citizen executive directors.
