  • Where: Bangor, Monson, and surrounding areas
    When: September 30-October 4, 2013

    FILM FORWARD partners with the Town of Monson, Mt. Desert Island High School, and Bangor High School for a first time program in Monson, Bangor and surrounding areas. FILM FORWARD will tour with Michael Gottwald (Producer, Beasts of the Southern Wild) and Laura Nix (Director, The Light In Her Eyes) to do a series of screenings, discussions and workshops. 

    Beasts of the Southern Wild tells the story of a forgotten but defiant bayou community cut off from the rest of the world by a sprawling levee where a six-year-old girl exists on the brink of orphan hood. The Light in Her Eyes tells the story of Houda al-Habash, a conservative Muslim teacher who challenges the women of her community to live according to Islam, without giving up their dreams.

    Other films that will be screening with this program but without filmmaker representatives are: Bones Brigade: An Autobiography, Chasing Ice, The Loving Story, Under The Same Moon (La Misma Luna), Town of Runners, and Valley of Saints.

    Town of Monson
    Monson is a small, rural community on the shores of Lake Hebron in central Maine. Monson is proud that black slate from the local quarry was used for the headstone markers of President John F. Kennedy and Mrs. Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis, at Arlington National Cemetery in Washington, DC.  Monson’s cultural heritage was once composed primarily of families of Finnish, Swedish and English decent who moved to the area to work in the slate quarries and timber industry. Some of the traditions from these early settlers are still reflected in the popular Finn dances held on summer Saturday nights at the local Finn Hall.  

    Mt. Desert High School
    Mount Desert Island High School is a comprehensive 4 year public high school with an enrollment of approximately 571 students, 70 faculty members, and 20 support staff. It is a Regional Community High School serving not only Mount Desert Island, but also 5 surrounding mainland communities and 4 outer island communities. It is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges and by the State of Maine.

    Bangor High School
    Among the largest comprehensive high schools in Maine, Bangor High School serves approximately 1,250 students in grades nine through twelve.  Our students have a history of distinguishing themselves as outstanding academic, athletic, and community leaders. With a state-leading number of National Merit Semifinalists, an Advanced Placement Program that consistently yields student performance above state and national levels, dozens of AP Scholars, and over 110 co- and extra-curricular state championships, the culture of achievement at our school is special. As a recent BHS graduate shared, “It’s cool to be smart here.”
